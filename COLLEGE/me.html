About the Department

The Mechanical Engineering Department at Abacus Institute offers a solid foundation in core mechanical sciences, including thermodynamics, robotics, fluid mechanics, and material science. The curriculum fosters problem-solving, innovation, and practical engineering skills.

Hands-on learning through workshops, industrial projects, and simulation labs empowers students to tackle real-world mechanical challenges.

Labs & Facilities

Thermal Engineering Lab

Focus on thermodynamics experiments and heat transfer simulations.

Fluid Mechanics & Machinery Lab

Study fluid flow, pumps, turbines, and machinery operation.

CAD/CAM Lab

Practical sessions for computer-aided design and manufacturing.

Strength of Materials Lab

Material testing, stress-strain analysis, and structural simulations.

Robotics & Mechatronics Lab

Hands-on training in robotics, automation, and mechatronic systems.

Student Activities

Students actively engage in design competitions, SAE events, workshops, and industrial training programs. The “Mech Masters Club” organizes technical events and mentorship programs.

Career Opportunities

Graduates are employed in sectors like automotive, aerospace, manufacturing, power generation, and R&D. Job roles include design engineer, maintenance engineer, production planner, and thermal analyst.
