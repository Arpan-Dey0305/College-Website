About the Department

The Department of Electronics & Communication Engineering at Abacus Institute builds a strong foundation in electronics fundamentals and modern communication systems. The curriculum covers digital signal processing, VLSI, embedded systems, and wireless technologies.

With a blend of theoretical knowledge and hands-on training, students are equipped to contribute to rapidly evolving electronics and communication industries.

Labs & Facilities

Embedded Systems Lab

Hands-on training on microcontrollers, IoT devices, and embedded platforms.

Communication Systems Lab

Experiments in wireless, optical, and digital communication systems.

VLSI Design & Simulation Lab

Learn chip design, FPGA programming, and circuit simulations.

Digital Signal Processing Lab

Hands-on experience with DSP algorithms, filters, and signal analysis.

Microprocessor & Microcontroller Lab

Practice programming and interfacing with microprocessor and microcontroller systems.

Student Activities

ECE students participate in IoT challenges, circuit hackathons, project expos, and technical seminars through the ECE Club and Institute Innovation Council.

Career Opportunities

ECE graduates pursue careers in telecom, embedded systems, automation, software development, and chip design industries. Recruiters include ISRO, DRDO, Qualcomm, and Infosys.
