About the Department

The Electrical Engineering Department at Abacus Institute offers a robust foundation in power systems, control engineering, power electronics, and electrical machines. The curriculum integrates theory with real-world applications.

Innovation, sustainability, and practical skills are emphasized to prepare students for success in industry and research.

Labs & Facilities

Power Systems Lab

Hands-on experiments on generation, transmission, and distribution of electricity.

Electrical Machines Lab

Practical learning of motors, transformers, and machine operations.

Control Systems Lab

Study of controllers, feedback systems, and automation techniques.

Power Electronics Lab

Hands-on training with converters, inverters, and electrical drives.

Simulation & Circuit Design Lab

Simulations and design of electrical circuits using software tools.

Student Activities

Students participate in technical workshops, IEEE events, paper presentations, and hardware competitions. The "VoltTech" club fosters innovation and collaborative learning.

Career Opportunities

Graduates work in power generation, transmission, automation, and renewable energy sectors. Career paths include electrical engineers, power system analysts, control engineers, and maintenance officers.
